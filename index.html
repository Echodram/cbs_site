Our Core Values

These foundational principles guide everything we do at the Center for Biblical Studies

Biblical Training

We provide comprehensive biblical education that equips students with knowledge and skills for faithful interpretation and application of Scripture in ministry contexts.

Research Library

Our extensive theological library offers access to thousands of resources, supporting in-depth study and research for students and scholars alike.

Christian Growth

We emphasize spiritual formation, helping students develop Christ-like character through discipleship, worship, and practical ministry experiences.

Church History

We preserve and teach the rich heritage of Christian thought and practice, connecting students to the historical roots of their faith.

Our Campuses

Our state-of-the-art campuses provide the perfect environment for spiritual growth and academic excellence. With modern facilities, prayer gardens, and dedicated study spaces, we've created spaces that foster both community and individual reflection.

We have three main campuses across the country, each offering unique opportunities for ministry training and cultural engagement. Our locations are strategically chosen to serve diverse communities while maintaining our commitment to biblical fidelity.

Meet Our Faculty

Our distinguished faculty bring decades of combined experience in ministry, scholarship, and teaching. They are committed to mentoring the next generation of Christian leaders.

Dr. Sarah Johnson

Professor of New Testament Studies

With over 20 years of teaching experience and numerous published works, Dr. Johnson specializes in Pauline theology and early Christian communities. She brings passion to every classroom discussion.

Dr. Michael Chen

Professor of Systematic Theology

A former pastor and now our resident theologian, Dr. Chen makes complex doctrines accessible and applicable. His courses on Christology and the Trinity are student favorites.

Dr. Robert Williams

Professor of Old Testament Studies

With expertise in Hebrew and Ancient Near Eastern studies, Dr. Williams illuminates the Old Testament with historical and linguistic insights that bring the text to life.
